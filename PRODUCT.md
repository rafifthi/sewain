# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is an owner or landlord managing rental operations across Indonesian property types such as kos, kontrakan, ruko, apartments, and custom unit models. They are usually checking the product during active operational work: reviewing occupancy, following up invoices, handling tenant issues, configuring payment or WhatsApp providers, and keeping documents or lease dates under control.

Secondary users include staff or admins who handle day-to-day operational tasks, while vendors and tenants are mostly future or lightweight touchpoints through assigned tickets, WhatsApp, public forms, payment links, and utility purchase flows.

## Product Purpose

Sewain is a property-management operations hub that keeps the path from vacant unit to paid invoice to resolved complaint in one place. It exists to help landlords run rentals without stitching together spreadsheets, chat threads, document folders, payment reminders, and maintenance notes.

Success means the owner can understand portfolio health quickly, spot money or occupancy issues before they become expensive, send or reconcile payments confidently, and move tenant requests through a clear operational workflow.

## Brand Personality

Efficient, clean, easy.

The product should feel calm and capable: direct enough for daily operational use, trustworthy around money and documents, and approachable for owners who need practical clarity more than enterprise theater.

## Anti-references

Do not make it feel like a flashy SaaS marketing page. Avoid decorative hero-like dashboard patterns, generic AI-looking card grids, overuse of gradients, ornamental motion, and visual treatments that compete with the operational task.

## Design Principles

1. Prioritize the next operational decision: every screen should make the owner’s next action obvious.
2. Keep financial and tenant information readable under repeated daily use.
3. Use familiar product affordances instead of novelty: standard navigation, tabs, forms, tables, and status language should behave predictably.
4. Treat integrations as serious configuration surfaces, even while providers are stubbed.
5. Preserve Indonesian property-management context without making the UI feel niche or fragile.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text, controls, focus states, and status indicators. Support reduced-motion users by keeping animation purposeful and non-blocking. Use readable type sizes, clear labels, visible focus treatment, and color plus text/icon cues for states so workflows remain usable for owners working through long operational sessions.
